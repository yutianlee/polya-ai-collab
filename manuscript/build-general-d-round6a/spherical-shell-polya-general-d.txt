                                                 Abstract
         For a spherical shell in dimension d ≥ 3, the separated radial problem has Bessel orders ℓ +
     (d − 2)/2 and spherical-harmonic multiplicities κd,ℓ . We give an exact branching decomposition
     of the strict shell-action proxy into positive integer- and half-integer shifted planar tails. We
     also prove a signed primitive comparison and retain its full positive remainder. This yields the
     exact identity

     Consequently, one dimension-free positive shifted-tail inequality implies the sharp Dirichlet
     Pólya inequality for every spherical shell and every d ≥ 3. The dimension lift is proved here; the
     inner shifted-tail inequality remains the central open lemma. We prove the outer and terminal
     tail regions, close the ﬁrst interface-cell milestone by exact rational wall estimates, and prove all
     shifted tails above an explicit ﬁxed-ratio high-energy threshold. A wall-safe ﬁrst-shelf reduction
     compresses every remaining many-cell tail to one scalar coupled to its terminal one-interface
     reserve. In the thin sector it also improves the uniform threshold to K ≥ 125/[8(1 − ρ)2 ].
     A scale-free shelf-curvature bound and a fractional-top inverse-action estimate give an explicit
     exact-angle lower bound for the terminal reserve, including the previously unused zero-level
     triangle. We also prove an automatic ultra-thin high-ﬂoor sector and exclude every noncom-
     pact escape in both the ﬁrst-ﬂoor and high-ﬂoor ﬁrst-drop branches. A quantitative transfer
     further excludes negative high-ﬂoor ﬁrst drops when 1 − ρ ≤ 10−8 and gives the explicit, though
     enormous, residual cutoﬀ K < 156250000000000000. The induced high-ﬂoor integer bounds are
     explicit but enormous, and the residual compact walls remain uncertiﬁed. A directed-rounding
     certiﬁcate proves all 19602 ﬁnite ﬁrst-ﬂoor chambers with r ≥ 3/2, s ∈ {0, 1}, x < 100. A second
     certiﬁcate handles all 398 ﬁnite small-start chambers, while an analytic comparison closes their
     inﬁnite rays. Together with the continuous large-ray certiﬁcate, this proves the entire ﬁrst-ﬂoor
     sector s ∈ {0, 1}. In the no-drop branch we prove every n = 1 case and reduce the remaining
     data to ﬁnitely many discrete chambers, with the explicit bound n < 380 in the two low-ﬂoor
     rows. These results do not prove every remaining compact chamber, and no assertion in this
     manuscript treats the full residual terminal-shelf scalar as proved.

9     Remaining proof obligations
The ﬁrst interface-cell milestone is complete, and Proposition 6.8 proves that for each ﬁxed ρ only
a bounded frequency interval remains; in the thin sector Proposition 6.10 improves the suﬃcient
threshold to K ≥ 125/[8(1 − ρ)2 ]. Proposition 7.1 further replaces the many-cell dynamics by the
single scalar (66). For the spectral theorem this scalar is needed only in (154). Lemma 7.2 sharpens
its head term, while Corollary 7.4 replaces qualitative terminal nonnegativity by a fractional-top
positive sum, and Corollary 7.5 covers the empty-level case. Proposition 7.14 closes an explicit high-
ﬂoor ﬁnite-shell sector, while Theorem 7.15 rules out a negative leading obstruction on the grazing
ray. For p = n, Proposition 7.12 identiﬁes the exact endpoint phase and Theorem 7.24 proves a
global ﬁnite reduction. It does not certify every member of that ﬁnite family. Theorems 7.10 and
7.17 remove every noncompact ﬁrst-drop escape. Theorem 7.18 further replaces the qualitative
high-ﬂoor bound by the explicit ranges (111)– (112); it does not certify the residual compact walls.
The primary route now has the following precise steps.
    1. In the no-drop branch, certify the residual one-sided walls in the explicit central and outer
       boxes (142)–(143), together with the two rows f = 2, 3, 2 ≤ n ≤ 379 from Theorem 7.23.
       The n = 1 branch is already closed by Corollary 7.21; in the remaining chambers the discard
       sector (133) and the favorable endpoint unit in (87) should be retained.
    2. For p < n, certify the compact residual wall sets left by Theorems 7.10 and 7.17. In the
       ﬁrst-ﬂoor branch, Theorems 7.6 and 7.7 already close the full sector r ≥ 3/2, s ∈ {0, 1},
       and Theorem 7.8 closes the two remaining starts. Thus only the compact s ≥ 2 residue
       remains on the ﬁrst ﬂoor. In the high-ﬂoor branch Theorem 7.18 gives explicit but enormous
       ﬁnite bounds for every integer label. The compact analytic walls inside those bounds are still
       uncertiﬁed; they require sharper analytic localization or directed-rounding certiﬁcates, not a
       claim of closure from ﬁniteness alone.
    3. Insert the resulting all-frequency shifted-tail theorem into Theorem 5.3.      No dimension-
       dependent angular or optical theorem is then needed.
