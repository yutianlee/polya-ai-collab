\begin{abstract}
This support volume contains the detailed hand proofs and exact-rational
tables used to remove every executable predicate from the logical dependency
chain of the spherical-shell theorem. Parts~I--VI retain the previously
audited 611-row exact ledger, including superseded rows as optional
cross-checks; Parts~VII--IX prove the retained-remainder theorem for every
frequency above the final staircase; and Part~X assembles the final residual
closure. The former aggregate-tail argument, programs, and interval
certificates remain available only as optional independent regression checks
and are not premises of this analytic proof.
\end{abstract}

\paragraph{Reading convention.}
The volume is modular so that each bottleneck lemma can be reviewed and
hashed independently. Every included part is generated from its corresponding
LaTeX source in the \texttt{manuscript/analytic} directory. The repeated title
pages deliberately mark the trust boundary between exhibits.

\section*{Global census of the exact ledger}
\addcontentsline{toc}{section}{Global census of the exact ledger}

The canonical ledger has \(553\) core rows and \(58\) supplemental rows.
The following inclusive ranges give a one-to-one allocation; the part numbers
refer to this volume.  The family codes denote, in order, rational-\(\pi\)
checks (PI), thresholds (TH), strict-floor conventions (SF), zero
specializations (Z), lower inventories (LI), checkpoint inventories (KI),
cap payments (CP), localizations (LOC), the upper owner (U), optical screens
(OPT), exact subtraction (SUB), and supplemental seam/payment rows (SUP).
\begin{center}
\small
\begin{tabular}{@{}lll@{}}
\toprule
canonical family&row range&unique owner\\
\midrule
PI&PI1--PI4&II\\
TH&TH1--TH7, TH31--TH34 / TH8--TH30&I / II\\
SF&SF1--SF35&II\\
Z&Z1--Z48&I\\
LI&LI1--LI8, LI10--LI44 / LI9&I / II\\
KI&KI1--KI117&II\\
CP&CP1--CP43, CP56, CP60--CP77 / remaining rows&II / III\\
LOC&LOC1--LOC47&III\\
U&U1--U8&V\\
OPT&OPT1--OPT14 / OPT15--OPT50&V / II\\
SUB&SUB1--SUB68&VI\\
SUP&SUP1--SUP57 / SUP58&IV / I\\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}
Here ``remaining rows'' in CP means CP44--CP55, CP57--CP59, and
CP78--CP98.  Thus the six disjoint owner totals are
\[
 103+278+83+57+22+68=611.
\]
In particular, the ten lower-\(d\) identities SUP36--SUP45 are assigned to
Part~IV although Part~II also proves their general odd-sum identity; SUP58
is assigned to Part~I; and OPT15--OPT50 are assigned to Part~II although
Part~V reuses their consequences.  These are proof redundancies, not row
overlaps.  The displayed ranges exhaust every canonical row and have empty
pairwise intersections.
