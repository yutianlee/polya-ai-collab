# Goals

- Maintain a claim-centered, auditable workflow for research on Pólya's
  conjecture.
- Preserve the internally proved sharp Dirichlet inequality for all genuine
  three-dimensional spherical shells.
- Keep the shell proof purely analytic: exact-rational and interval programs
  are regression checks, not theorem premises.
- Present the proof as a concise main paper plus one self-contained analytic
  support module.
- Keep the ellipse/Mathieu and certificate-family programs separate from the
  completed shell-class theorem.

## Current milestone

On 17 July 2026, the recommendations in `human/inbox/revision_1.md`,
`human/inbox/revision_2.md`, and `human/inbox/revision_2_2.md` were
implemented. The live low/middle-ratio argument now combines the
ratio-dependent angular slope cap with the tangent-envelope radial minorant.
This removes every finite staircase, state enumeration, and numerical ledger
from the theorem path.

The current final cover is:

- no mode for \(K\leq\pi/(1-\rho)\);
- one ratio-sharp defect theorem for \(K>\pi/(1-\rho)\) and
  \(0<\rho<39/50\); and
- the optical theorem for \(K>\pi/(1-\rho)\) and
  \(39/50\leq\rho<1\).

The main paper and support file compile, the verification boundary is exact,
and independent internal audits have reconstructed the angular, radial/ball,
scalar-convexity, exact-fraction, source-reference, and packaging components.
A flattened self-contained review bundle is available under
`output/submission/`. This remains an internal result for spherical shells,
not a solution of the general Pólya conjecture.
