# A. Evaluation of the shifted-tail dimension-lifting strategy

## A.1 What is correct ‚Äî and two things that are better than claimed

**The algebra is exact, not asymptotic.** $\sum_\ell \kappa_{d,\ell}s^\ell=(1-s^2)(1-s)^{-d}=\frac{1+s}{(1-s)^{d-1}}$ is correct, and the factorization is the $(d-2)$-fold iterate of the $SO(d)\!\downarrow\!SO(d-1)$ branching rule with $d=2$ as the atom. Crucially, the induced decomposition is **grid-exact**: with $c_{d,m}=\binom{m+d-3}{d-3}$ and shifts $r_m=m+\frac{d-2}{2}$, the shifted planar channels $\{r_m,r_m+1,\dots\}$ land *exactly* on the $d$-dimensional $\nu$-grid, so
$$P_d=\sum_{m\ge0}c_{d,m}\,T(r_m),\qquad T(r):=\Big[A(r)+\tfrac14\Big]_<+2\sum_{j\ge1}\Big[A(r{+}j)+\tfrac14\Big]_<,$$
with **no interpolation error whatsoever**. **[verified as an integer identity, $d=3..6$, multiple $(\rho,K)$.]** The weights $1,2,2,\dots$ are trapezoidal weights ‚Äî that is *why* $2\int_r^K A$ is the right continuous partner (the weight-1 first channel cancels the Euler‚ÄìMaclaurin half-term).

**The Weyl side is not merely controllable ‚Äî it is monotone in the right direction.** Setting $S_d(z)=\sum_{r_m<z}c_{d,m}$ and $\Delta_d(z)=\int_0^z\!\big(S_d(u)-\tfrac{u^{d-2}}{(d-2)!}\big)du$, summation by parts gives
$$\sum_m c_{d,m}\cdot 2\!\int_{r_m}^K\!\!A\;=\;W_d\;+\;2\!\int_0^K\!\!A\,\big(S_d-\tfrac{z^{d-2}}{(d-2)!}\big)dz\;=\;W_d-2\!\int_0^K|A'(z)|\,|\Delta_d(z)|\,dz\;\le\;W_d,$$
because $\Delta_d(z)\le0$ for **all** $z$. At grid points this is literally **AM‚ÄìGM**: $\Delta_d(r_Z)=\frac1{(d-1)!}\big[\prod_{i=0}^{d-2}(Z{+}i)-(Z{+}\tfrac{d-2}2)^{d-1}\big]\le0$, the mean of $\{Z,\dots,Z{+}d{-}2\}$ being exactly $r_Z$. **[grid points: proved, one line; continuum: verified numerically $d=3..8$ ‚Äî for $d\ge4$ strictly negative, for $d=3$, $\Delta_3\in[-\tfrac18,0]$ with equality only at integer midpoints; a full proof via per-cell maxima at the geometric mean, or induction on the branching partial sums, is a clean small lemma.]** So the convolution step needs **no error absorption at all** and even donates a small strict bonus. This is the single most elegant ingredient of the whole route, and the proposal did not notice it.

**Empirical health of the shifted inequality.** Sweeping all shifts $r\in\frac1{10}\mathbb Z$ at $(\rho,K)\in\{(0.5,40),(0.2,30),(0.8,50),(0.3,8)\}$: no violation of $(*)$ anywhere, and moreover the margin tracks the *linear law* $2\int_r^KA-T(r)\ \ge\ \frac{\pi-2}{2\pi}(K-r)-0.18$ ‚Äî a uniform-in-shift linear margin with only $O(1)$ slack, even through the turning window (which is protected by the strict bracket: $T(r)=0$ whenever $G_K(r)\le\frac34$, i.e. $K-r\lesssim1.77K^{1/3}$). The linear coefficient has a clean provenance: the shifted quarter-layer harvest is $\frac{K-r}{2}$, the layer count obeys $E^{(r)}\le A(r)+\frac14$, and $G_K(r)\le\frac{K-r}{\pi}$ **[proved this session: $\sqrt{1-s^2}-s\arccos s\le 1-s$ on $[0,1]$]**, giving the uniform coefficient $\frac12-\frac1\pi=\frac{\pi-2}{2\pi}\approx0.1817$.

## A.2 What is wrong or overclaimed ‚Äî the referee corrections

**(C1) $(*)$ as stated is the wrong target: it must be upgraded to a margin form.** As written, $(*)$ carries no reserve. It happens that, combined with the sign of $\Delta_d$, bare $(*)$ *would* already imply strict P√≥lya ‚Äî but with a margin of only $O(K^{d-2})\times$(small), far too thin to absorb any honest per-shift error, and a *proof* of bare $(*)$ at all scales secretly contains the full difficulty (its own internal margins). Worse, the $r=0$ instance of bare $(*)$ **is** proxy-P√≥lya in $d=2$ ‚Äî a statement *stronger* than $N_D\le W$ (since $P_2\ge N_D$), unproven at low frequency, and precisely where the FLPS void lives. The correct centerpiece is:

$$\boxed{\;2\int_r^K A(z)\,dz-T(r)\ \ge\ \frac{\pi-2}{2\pi}\,(K-r)\;-\;\Xi(r;\rho,K)\;}\tag{STL}$$
for all real $r\ge0$, with a certified error budget $\Xi$ that is **localized**: $O(1)$ only in the turning band $K-r\asymp K^{1/3}$, an inner-endpoint term $O(\sqrt{\rho K/(1-\rho)})$ only for $r<\rho K$, and small elsewhere; plus the trivial zone $T(r)=0$ for $K-r\le c_4K^{1/3}$.

**(C2) "Positive convolution gives every dimension at once" hides a moderate-$K$ gap.** A *flat* per-shift slack $\xi_0$ costs $\xi_0 S_d(K)\approx\xi_0\frac{K^{d-2}}{(d-2)!}$ against the margin $\frac{\pi-2}{2\pi}\frac{K^{d-1}}{(d-1)!}$, i.e. a threshold $K\gtrsim 5.5\,\xi_0(d-1)$ ‚Äî **linear in $d$** ‚Äî while the walls only cover $K\le\max\{\pi/(1-\rho),\ \nu_{\min}+O(\nu_{\min}^{1/3})\}\approx d/2$. Contrast: the direct route's interface costs carried a factor $\rho^{d-2}$ and stayed $d$-uniform. Closures exist (A.3/plan), the cleanest being $\Xi$-localization: slack concentrated in the turning band has $c_{d,m}$-weighted mass only $O(K^{d-3+1/3})$, restoring a $d$-free threshold. But as proposed, "all $d$ at once" is not yet true at moderate $K$.

**(C3) "May simultaneously yield a new proof in $d=2$" ‚Äî no.** $d=2$ is the *atom* of the lift, not an output; every hard feature of $d=2$ (the $O(1)$ scale-free angular noise, the low-$K$ void, $g_2(T)=\infty$) lives *inside* STL at small shifts. The lifted paper cites FLPS for $d=2$ exactly as before.

**(C4) The angular worst case is conserved, not dissolved.** At a fixed radial layer $R_n$, the fractional parts $\{R_n-r_m\}$ are **independent of $m$** (integer shift steps!), so there is no cancellation across shifts: $\sum_m c_{d,m}E^{(m)}$ reproduces the direct route's $E_{\mathrm{ang}}$ exactly, worst case and all. The block-gain content (R2's O-d1, slope floor $\pi/\theta$) must still be proved ‚Äî once, uniformly in $d$ ‚Äî either inside STL's error budget or as a separate convolution-level lemma. No free lunch here, as expected; the lift *repackages* this layer.

**(C5) One more gain the proposal missed.** Any $\nu$-level count lifts through the same convolution: $\#_d(x)=\sum_mc_{d,m}\#_2^{(r_m)}(x)$. Hence the **optical/product screens also lift** to shifted-planar screen lemmas (thresholds $x_n=\sqrt{(b_n/\varepsilon)^2+\nu_{\min}^2}$, mild $d$-dependence) ‚Äî potentially taming O-d4, the direct route's biggest labor item.

## A.3 Verdict

**Adopt, in the repaired (STL + sign-lemma + block-lemma) form, as the organizing architecture of the general-$d$ paper.** Compared with my R2 direct route it *strictly dominates* on the radial layer (one shifted planar Stieltjes argument replaces per-$d$ TV lemmas ‚Äî the proposal's claim 2, which is true and is the main technical gain) and on the Weyl/closure layer (AM‚ÄìGM sign replaces per-$d$ binomial certificates); it is *neutral* on the angular layer (C4); it *plausibly helps* the optical layer (C5); and it introduces *one new risk* ‚Äî the moderate-$K$ window (C2) ‚Äî with three credible closures. The positioning claim I endorse fully: "dimension lifting via harmonic branching positivity, reducing P√≥lya in every dimension to one one-parameter planar lattice inequality plus one AM‚ÄìGM lemma" is a paper-defining frame at the JEMS/Duke level, and both STL and the sign lemma are genuinely reusable, independent-interest objects (the sign lemma alone ‚Äî *the branching lift of the trapezoid count never exceeds its Weyl integral* ‚Äî is quotable). Keep the R2 direct route in the repository as the audited fallback for any layer that stalls. Confidence that the repaired strategy closes: $\approx0.7$ overall (same as R2's, but with a better paper if it lands); dominated by L4 and the window audit.
