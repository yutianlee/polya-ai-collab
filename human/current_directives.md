# Current Directives

## Authoritative theorem boundary

Preserve the project-internal, purely analytic proof of the sharp Dirichlet
Pólya inequality for every genuine three-dimensional spherical shell
\(A_{r,R}\), \(0<r<R\). The result is not a theorem for general domains and
does not carry a novelty, priority, peer-review, or publication claim.

## Live proof architecture

Use exactly the following disjoint unit-shell cover:

1. \(K\leq \pi/(1-\rho)\): the strict count is zero.
2. \(K>\pi/(1-\rho)\) and \(0<\rho<39/50\): use the ratio-sharp global
   defect theorem.
3. \(K>\pi/(1-\rho)\) and \(39/50\leq\rho<1\): use the optical theorem.

The shared ratio face \(\rho=39/50\) belongs to the optical owner, and the
frequency face \(K=\pi/(1-\rho)\) belongs to the no-mode owner.

The ratio-sharp theorem must retain:

- the exact strict layer cake and angular-wall convention;
- \(q\leq\arccos(\rho)/\pi\);
- the tangent envelope \(L_\sharp\leq\mathfrak W\);
- the scope \(\tau>1/4\) before invoking the \(g(\tau)/8\) reduction;
- nonnegativity of the ball-inverse lower bound before squaring; and
- the displayed exact rational convexity margins.

The former \(\rho_*\) split, \(k_6,\ldots,k_{11}\), 38-state theorem,
\(\mathcal D_{16},\ldots,\mathcal D_{20}\), 611-row ledger, interval
certificates, and executable event replays are historical or optional
regression evidence only. They must not be cited as premises of the revised
proof.

## Verification standard

After a mathematical source edit:

1. rebuild the main paper, ratio-sharp module, and supplement;
2. run graph validation and the ratio-sharp exact regression tests;
3. scan the live TeX for missing references and retired dependencies;
4. inspect rendered title, theorem, global-closure, final-assembly, and support
   pages; and
5. synchronize release PDFs and hashes.

Computation may check transcription and exact arithmetic, but it is not a
premise of the analytic theorem.
