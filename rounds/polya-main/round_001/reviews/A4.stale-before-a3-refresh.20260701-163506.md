## Possible errors or hidden assumptions

- **Branch and continuity of $\theta_\nu$.** A2's monotonicity proof assumes a globally continuous phase with $\theta_\nu(0^+)=-\pi/2$ (or some fixed limit). This is a standard construction, but it must be stated explicitly; otherwise the floor-sum identity may be off by a fixed shift if an inconsistent branch is chosen. The rigorous definition $\theta_\nu(z)=\arctan(Y_\nu(z)/J_\nu(z))$ with the usual analytic continuation works for all $z>0$, but the small-argument limit must be checked to guarantee $\Psi_{\nu,\rho}(0^+)=0$.

- **Floor-count and strict convention.** A2's exact formula $\#\{n\ge1: n\pi<\Psi\}$ correctly counts zeros **strictly** below $\sqrt\Lambda$. The upper bound $\lfloor\Psi/\pi\rfloor$ is valid, but at points where $\Psi$ is an integer multiple of $\pi$, the true count is one less than the floor, so the bound may overcount. For an upper bound that is not a problem, but any later attempt to claim equality would require handling these measure-zero sets.

- **A2's obstruction 1 (Dirichlet-bracketing)** relies on the fact that adding a Dirichlet wall (the spherical interface) increases eigenvalues, hence $N_{B_\rho}+N_A\le N_{B_1}$. While true, this step should be flagged as depending on the **variational principle for mixed boundary conditions**, which is standard but might need a citation. The conclusion (the bracketing bound overshoots the target) is correct.

- **A3's blocking of `ELLIPSE-near-circular` and `CERT-certificate-family` as "blocked"** is **too aggressive** without a completed source audit. The human directives explicitly keep the ellipse as a parallel flagship and the certificate family as a fallback.
