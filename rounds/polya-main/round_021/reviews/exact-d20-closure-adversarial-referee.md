## 3. Compact spectral bridge and finite-leaf implication

\[
N_D\leq P_{\rm coarse}

## 4. Aggregate low-tail identities and strict spectral implication

Write

\[
\mu-\frac12=J+\tau,qquad J\in\mathbb N_0,qquad0\leq\tau<1.
\]

For low-tail starts \(x_r=r+1/2<\mu\), the two wall cases are exact:

- if \(\tau=0\), then \(R=J\) and \(r=0,\ldots,J-1\);
- if \(0<\tau<1\), then \(R=J+1\), with the additional \(n_J=0\) cell.

In both cases

\[
n_r=\lfloor\mu-x_r\rfloor=J-r,qquad
q_r=x_r+n_r=J+\frac12,
\]

so

\[
\sum_{r<R}n_r=\frac{J(J+1)}2,qquad
\mu-q_r=\tau.
\tag{9}
\]

Thus every low tail has the same interface loss

\[
0\leq\delta_\tau\leq
\frac{2\tau^{5/2}}{15\sqrt\mu},
\]

with exact zero at \(\tau=0\).  The accepted curvature estimate gives for
the first-shelf length

\[
p_r^2+2x_rp_r<C,qquad
p_r<\sqrt{x_r^2+C}-x_r.
\tag{10}
\]

The right side is strictly convex in \(x\).  Midpoint
Hermite--Hadamard on every unit interval has the required direction and
therefore

\[
\sum_{r<R}p_r<\mathcal I(C,R),
\]

where

\[
\mathcal I(C,R)=\frac12\left[
R\sqrt{R^2+C}+C\operatorname{arsinh}\frac R{\sqrt C}-R^2
\right].
\tag{11}
\]

Summing the accepted one-tail estimate gives the reserve

\[
\begin{aligned}
\mathcal Q={}&R\lfloor K\eta_\rho\rfloor
+d_\rho\frac{J(J+1)}2
-(1+d_\rho)\mathcal I(C,R)\\
&-\frac{8R\tau^{5/2}}{15\sqrt\mu}.
\end{aligned}
\tag{12}

The factor 8 is necessary and correct: it is four times the factor 2 in the
interface-loss bound because each tail estimate contains \(-M_r/4\).  The
strict shelf sum yields
\(\sum M_r>\mathcal Q+8R\tau^{5/2}/(15\sqrt\mu)\).  Hence
\(\mathcal Q\geq0\) makes the total low-tail error strictly negative.
The strictness survives \(\tau=0\), where the interface term vanishes but

All starts \(x_r\geq\mu\), including equality at a half-integer inner
