# Adversarial Review: Imported Global Shell Phase Enclosure

Verdict: PASS.

The reviewer reconstructed all identities and checked them against the permitted source cards.

- The derivative

$$
\Psi_{\nu,\rho}'(K)=\frac{2}{\pi K}
\left(M_\nu(K)^{-2}-M_\nu(\rho K)^{-2}\right)
$$

is positive because $M_\nu^2$ strictly decreases.
- The small- and large-$K$ limits are correct for fixed $\nu$.
- FLPS annuli Lemma 5.2 and $F_\mu\ge-\tfrac14$ give the global bound with the displayed inequality directions.
- Equation (5.8) includes $\nu=\mu$ and does not use the divergent $H_\mu$ there.
- On $\mu<\nu\le K$, only the global one-sided bound is justified.
- The repository determinant has $+\sin\Psi$ and is the negative of the annulus determinant.
- Positive zeros are indexed by $\Psi(K_n)=n\pi$, $n\ge1$.
- The strict count is

$$
\#\{n\ge1:n\pi<\Psi(K)\}
=\left\lceil\frac{\Psi(K)}{\pi}\right\rceil-1,
$$

so an exact floor is wrong at positive phase endpoints.

Residual limitations:

- the large-$K$ expansion is not uniform in $\nu$ or $\rho$;
- the mixed region has no correlated $\Phi$ bound;
- the absolute $1/4$ slack remains uncontrolled after $2\ell+1$-weighted summation and may dominate as $\rho\to1$;
- the lemma is a phase/root enclosure, not a proof of the shell theorem.
